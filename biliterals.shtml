BILITERALS

Biliteral signs represent two consonants, which might or might not have been separated by one or more vowels. At least they're both in the same word.
Some signs represent more than one set of consonants. Some consonant pairs have more than one sign.
The list shown on this page is NOT exhaustive, but it does contain the most common biliteral signs.
Beneath each sign are listed its English version of MdC and its Gardiner Code. With MdC, as usual, upper and lower case are significant.
